I am an associate professor of statistics at the University of Michigan and a (part-time) researcher at the MBZUAI Institute of Foundation Models (IFM). My research leverages statistical science to make AI safer and more reliable. Some topics of recent interest include

  • AI evaluation,
  • algorithmic fairness,
  • transfer learning.

Before coming to Michigan, I obtained my PhD and BA in computational math from Stanford University, advised by Michael Saunders and Jonathan Taylor, and Rice University respectively. I was born in Hangzhou, but I spent my formative years in Singapore and the SF Bay Area.
