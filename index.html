About

I am an assistant professor in the statistics department at the University of Michigan. My research is guided by the statistical and computational challenges in machine learning. Some topics of recent interest are

  • algorithmic fairness,
  • federated learning,
  • learning in non-IID settings.

More broadly, I am interested in the mathematical foundations of data science. I obtained my PhD in computational mathematics from Stanford University, where I worked with Michael Saunders and Jonathan Taylor.

I have learned a lot in my life. Unfortunately, most of it is about technicalities. – JR
